Deforestation in the Amazon has reached a 15 year high

Home to about three million species of plants and animals, the Amazon Rainforest is the largest rainforest in the world. At the current rate of deforestation, 27% of the Amazon will be without trees by 2030. We are promoting awareness of deforestation to help conservationists in their fight against rainforest decay. This image recognition model trained on satellite images of the Amazon will be able to determine the level of deforestation on new satellite photographs.

Primary Causes of Deforestation

Beef and soy are the leading drivers of tropical deforestation, comprising over two-thirds of the recorded habitat loss. Increasing infrastructure, such as road building, have a large indirect effect through opening up forests to settlers and agriculture. This gives way for mining and illegal wood harvesting. Natural forest fires which take advantage of vulnerable degraded forests. Climate change is both a cause and effect of deforestation.

What Can be Done?

Reducing your use in wood, paper, oil and beef can help fight deforestation. Logging companies continue to cut down endangered forests due to the high demand in products. Holding these businesses accountable is an important factor in rainforest conservation. Consider getting involved in organizations leading the fight against deforestation.
